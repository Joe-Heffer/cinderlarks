Heavy rooms, soft songs

Some albums are photographs. This one is a pressed flower kept in a borrowed book — small, warm, easy to miss unless you're looking. The Cinderlarks write in the shadow of Sheffield's old steelworks: songs that stay gentle even when the walls around them are rust and rivets.

Four voices, one room, first takes kept.

Tape, not templates

Everything's cut to reel-to-reel before it ever touches a computer. Room mics, first takes, the hiss left in on purpose — the process is as unpolished as the songs are tender.
