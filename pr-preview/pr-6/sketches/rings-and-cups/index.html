Cups and Rings

Somewhere on a moor, someone knelt at a stone and carved a ring around a hollow, and then another ring, and another, for reasons nobody wrote down. Cup-and-ring marks are read as offerings — devotion given without explanation, made again and again until it wore a groove into rock. This is a song about loving someone the same unremarkable way: for you, for you, for you.
