The Kid Who Drowned

There's a stretch of the reservoir where the water goes glass-still at dusk, and my dad used to say you could tell how deep a thing was by how little it moved. I didn't believe him then. I still don't, not really — but I think about it every time I stand at the edge of something and it doesn't ripple back. This one isn't about tragedy. It's about the moment the pulling stops feeling like a fight.
